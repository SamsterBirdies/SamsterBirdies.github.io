Good file formats

List of good containers and codecs with pros and cons of each.
If something is not on here, then either it's new or probably sucks lol.

List of lists:

- Audio
- Image
- Video
- Text

Audio:

Typically all audio formats will sound the same, because they are usually stored with a bitrate high enough for transparency.

.mp3

Lossy audio to use when wide compatibility is a must.

A lossy format released in 1991. Because of its age, basically everything will accept mp3. However, that also means its compression is very dated and has poor compression.
For me, it sounds transparent at 192kbps. 128kbps and below starts having nasty artifacts with higher frequencies.

Pros:

  • Very widely compatible.

Cons:

  • Inefficient lossy compression.

.wav

Wide compatibility and with no compression.

Usually contains Linear PCM audio.

Pros:

  • Lossless audio quality.
  • Very widely compatible.

Cons:

  • No compression means large file sizes.

.ogg

Good compression lossy codec to use when Opus is not supported.

Contains Vorbis codec released in 2000. It is lossy and has good compression.
The Vorbis codec is superseded by the Opus codec. However, Vorbis has higher compatibility at the moment.
I think it sounds transparent at about Q3 (112kbps). However, Vorbis does not handle square waves well and will sound noisy with them.

Pros:

  • Good compression
  • Decent compatibility

Cons:

  • Not as widely supported as .mp3
  • Superseded by Opus

.flac

Good lossless compressed audio codec.

Free Lossless Audio Codec was released in 2001. This codec offers lossless compression. It has good compatibility, but not as much as .wav.

Pros:

  • Lossless compression
  • Decent compatibility

Cons:

  • Not as widely supported as .wav

.opus

Best lossy compression.

Opus codec currently has the best lossy compression. It was released in 2012.
This is the codec used often in audio streaming services.
It sounds very good, even at 64kbps.
Unfortunately, it is not widely compatible since it is relatively new. Ogg Vorbis is a good alternative.

Pros:

  • Amazing compression

Cons:

  • Low compatibility due to its recency.
